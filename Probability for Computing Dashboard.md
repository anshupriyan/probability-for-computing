# Probability for Computing Dashboard

Welcome to your study dashboard for **DSC 06: Probability for Computing**. Use this space to plan your study sessions, track your progress through the syllabus, and quickly navigate to your notes.

---

## 🗺️ Syllabus & Learning Roadmap

Use this checklist to track your understanding of each topic. Mark items with `[x]` as you master them!

### 🟩 Unit 1: Basic Probability
- [ ] **Fundamentals**: Notion of probability, random experiments, sample spaces, and events.
- [ ] **Algebra of Events**: Set operations on events, definition of probability on events.
- [ ] **Conditional Probability**: Computing conditional probabilities and understanding independent events.
- [ ] **Bayes' Theorem**: Formulating and applying Bayes' Theorem.

### 🟦 Unit 2: Random Variables
- [ ] **Foundations**: Random Variables, Probability Mass/Density Functions (PMF/PDF), Cumulative Distribution Functions (CDF).
- [ ] **Discrete Random Variables**: Bernoulli, Binomial, Poisson, Multinomial, Geometric, **Negative Binomial, and Hypergeometric** distributions.
- [ ] **Continuous Random Variables**: Uniform, Exponential, Normal, **Beta, and Gamma** distributions.
- [ ] **Expectation, Variance & Moments**: Expected value, expectation of functions, variance, **moments, and Moment Generating Functions (MGFs)**.
- [ ] **Inequalities**: Markov inequality and Chebyshev's inequality.
- [ ] **Limit Theorems**: Weak and Strong Laws of Large Numbers, Central Limit Theorem (CLT - **Lindeberg-Levy & De-Moivre Laplace**).

### 🟨 Unit 3: Joint Distributions
- [ ] **Joint Variables**: Jointly distributed random variables and joint distribution functions.
- [ ] **Independence**: Conditions for independence of multiple random variables.
- [ ] **Covariance & Correlation**: Calculating covariance and Karl Pearson's correlation coefficients.
- [ ] **Conditional Expectation**: Expected value of a random variable conditioned on another.

### 🟪 Unit 4: Markov Chain and Information Theory
- [ ] **Stochastic Processes**: Introduction to stochastic systems and processes.
- [ ] **Chapman-Kolmogorov**: Chapman-Kolmogorov equations.
- [ ] **State Classification**: Transient, recurrent, and absorbing states.
- [ ] **Limiting Probabilities**: Solving for limiting and stationary probability distributions.
- [ ] **Random Number Generation**: Techniques for generating pseudo-random numbers.
