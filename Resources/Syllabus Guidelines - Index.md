## 📖 Syllabus Overview & Reference Mapping

The course follows the primary textbook **[1] Sheldon M. Ross, *Introduction to Probability Models*, 10th Edition, Elsevier, 2019**.

### 🟩 Unit 1: Basic Probability
* **Topics Covered**: Introduction to the notion of probability, Random experiment, Sample space and Events, Probability defined on events, Algebra of events. Conditional probabilities, independent events, Bayes’ theorem.
* **Textbook Reference**: Book **[1]** Sections `1.1`, `1.2`, `1.3`, `1.4`, `1.5`, `1.6` (Reading PDF: [[unit1.pdf]])
* **Study Notes**: *(Create notes for these topics as you progress)*

### 🟦 Unit 2: Random Variables
* **Topics Covered**: Introduction to Random Variables, Probability mass/density functions, Cumulative distribution functions. Discrete Random Variables (Bernoulli, Binomial, Poisson, Multinomial and Geometric). Continuous Random Variables (Uniform, Exponential and Normal). Expectation of a Random Variable, Expectation of Function of a Random Variable and Variance. Markov inequality, Chebyshev’s inequality, Central Limit Theorem, Weak and Strong Laws of Large Numbers.
* **Textbook Reference**: Book **[1]** Sections `2.1`, `2.2`, `2.3` (Excluding 2.3.3), `2.4`, `2.8`
* **Study Notes**: *(Create notes for these topics as you progress)*

### 🟨 Unit 3: Joint Distributions
* **Topics Covered**: Jointly distributed Random Variables, Joint distribution functions, Independent Random Variables, Covariance of Random Variables, Correlation Coefficients, Conditional Expectation.
* **Textbook Reference**: Book **[1]** Sections `2.5.1`, `2.5.2`, `2.5.3`, `3.1`, `3.2`, `3.3`, `3.4`
* **Study Notes**: *(Create notes for these topics as you progress)*

### 🟪 Unit 4: Markov Chain and Information Theory
* **Topics Covered**: Introduction to Stochastic Processes, Chapman–Kolmogorov equations, Classification of states, Limiting and Stationary Probabilities. Random Number Generation, Pseudo Random Numbers.
* **Textbook Reference**: Book **[1]** Sections `4.1`, `4.2`, `4.3` (Till Example 4.17), `4.4` (Till Example 4.22), `11.1` (Pages 667-669)
* **Study Notes**: *(Create notes for these topics as you progress)*
